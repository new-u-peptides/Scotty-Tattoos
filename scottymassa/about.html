About

Ten years, line by line.

I started tattooing in 2014 with a hand-poke kit and a stack of geometry books. A decade later, the brief hasn't changed — fewer pieces, drawn slow, made to outlast a trend.

My work is mostly about composition — how a shape sits on the body, how the body carries it across the years.

Story

How I got here.

I apprenticed under a hand-poke artist in Lisbon in my early twenties — first as a guest in their kitchen, then as a proper apprentice. The geometry obsession came later, when a client asked for a Sri Yantra and I spent three weeks studying it before I felt ready to draw it.

Today I work out of Massa Tattoo — a small, private studio I helped open in 2019. I take a handful of clients a week. I don't take walk-ins.
