§ 01 — About

A small studio, quiet on purpose.

Massa Tattoo is a private, appointment-only studio. We take a handful of clients each week so every piece gets the attention it needs — from the first conversation to the last line.

Book a consult →

We believe a tattoo should outlast a trend. Most of what we draw is drawn slow — sketched, set down, picked back up — until the line finds its final shape.

§ 02 — Story

How the studio came together.

What started in a back room with a hand-poke kit and a stack of geometry books grew into a quiet, full-time studio. The brief never changed: do fewer pieces, do them well, and treat every client like they brought you something worth getting right.

Today the studio works out of a small space on Atelier Lane — natural light, warm wood, three chairs, and a portfolio that keeps a single thread through ten years of work.

  1. 2014

    Hand-poked beginnings

    Massa apprentices under a hand-poke artist in Lisbon, taking her first appointments at twenty-two.

  2. 2017

    First machine work

    Transitions to machine work; develops the dotwork style the studio is now known for.

  3. 2019

    Studio opens

    Massa Tattoo opens on Atelier Lane — two chairs, a guest spot, and a waitlist that fills three months out.

  4. 2022

    Two residents join

    Two residents join — one specialising in fine line, one in blackwork — broadening the studio's range without diluting its voice.

  5. Today

    1,200+ pieces in

    Still small, still booked out, still drawing every piece by hand before it ever sees a stencil.

§ 04 — Process

Four steps from idea to ink.

01

Conversation

You send the idea through the form — symbolism, placement, scale. We come back with the right artist and a draft direction.

02

Sketch

The artist sketches your piece by hand, then refines until the composition feels resolved. Two rounds of revisions are baked in.

03

Sitting

You come in, we stencil, we tweak placement until it lives right on the body. Then we make the marks.

04

Aftercare

You leave with a wrap, an aftercare sheet, and a number to text if anything looks off. We check in after two weeks.
