## How to shoot good portraits for the site

A few notes since this category really matters:

- **Catch-light in the eyes.** A small reflection from a window or light
  source in the pupils makes a portrait read alive. Avoid harsh overhead
  light that flattens the face into shadow.
- **Slight angle, not flat-front.** A 3/4 turn of the body with the face
  toward camera is more dynamic than a straight-on shot.
- **Studio backdrop, not a white wall.** The wood, the brick, the flash
  wall, the black subway tile — they're all on-brand. A neutral white
  wall reads like a passport photo. Wear black, let the warm wood pop
  behind.
- **Wear what he'd actually wear in the chair.** Black tee, gloves on
  the table next to him, machine in frame somewhere — read instantly as
  "the artist", not "a person who happens to be there".
- **Shoot at f/2.8 – f/4** if you have control. Sharp on the face, soft
  in the background. Avoid `f/1.4` selfie-mode portraits — too shallow
  and the ears go soft.
- **Multiple frames per setup.** Send 3–5 variants per setup, I'll pick
  the strongest.
- **Vertical (4:5)** is the most useful aspect — works in heroes, splits,
  cards, social. If in doubt, shoot vertical.
