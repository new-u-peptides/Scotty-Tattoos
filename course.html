The Curriculum

Nine phases. Zero guesswork.

The same curriculum I teach in person, in the order it needs to be learned. Each phase builds on the last — skip one and it shows up later in your work.

01 / 09

Portfolio Audit

We start with an honest look at where your art actually stands — what's strong, what needs work, and what translates to skin.

02 / 09

Mindset

Thinking like a working artist, not a hobbyist. How professionals approach practice, criticism, clients and the long game.

03 / 09

Equipment & Setup

Machines, needles, cartridges, power — what you actually need, how to set it up, and how to cut through the kit confusion.

04 / 09

Core Techniques

The physics of tattooing — how the needle interacts with skin. Depth, angle, speed and stretch, and the why behind each one.

05 / 09

Application

Lines, shading and packing on practice skin — building consistency and control before anything touches a real client.

06 / 09

Hygiene & Safety

Studio-grade cross-contamination control, station setup and breakdown. Non-negotiable, and taught to professional standard.

07 / 09

Integration

Bringing your own art style into tattooing — turning what you already draw into designs that work on a body and age well.

08 / 09

Structured Practice

A practice system with direct feedback on your work — on practice skin and real tattoos — so mistakes get corrected, not repeated.

09 / 09

Studio Entry

Building a portfolio that gets you taken seriously, and the strategy for walking into a studio as a working artist.

FAQ

Questions people ask before joining.

If yours isn't here, message me on Instagram and ask directly.

How much does the online tattoo mentorship cost?

The mentorship is €497 as a one-time payment. That covers the full 9-phase curriculum, group mentorship, direct feedback on your work and lifetime access — including new video content as it's added. There's no subscription and no upsells, and it's covered by a 7-day money-back guarantee for students who genuinely engage with the course.

Do I need tattooing experience to join?

No. The mentorship is built to take you from zero tattooing experience to portfolio-ready work. What matters is artistic ability and the willingness to practise — the course starts with an audit of where your art actually stands, then builds the tattooing on top of it phase by phase.

Is the course live or self-paced?

The curriculum is self-paced — you work through the nine phases on your own schedule, with lifetime access. Around it sits group mentorship: you submit your work, on practice skin and later on real tattoos, and get direct feedback so mistakes get corrected before they become habits.

How is this different from learning off YouTube?

YouTube gives you scattered techniques with no order and nobody checking your work. The mentorship gives you a structured path — portfolio audit, equipment, core mechanics, application, hygiene, practice, studio entry — and teaches the why behind each technique: how the needle actually interacts with skin. Then it adds the thing tutorials can't: feedback on your own work.

Is this the same as the in-person courses at Massa Tattoo in Malta?

Same foundations, different format. The online mentorship covers the same 9-phase curriculum taught in person, delivered online so you can join from anywhere in the world. If you'd rather train hands-on at the studio in Birkirkara, Massa Tattoo also runs small-group in-person courses.

How long until I'm producing portfolio-ready work?

Students who follow the structured practice phases are typically producing portfolio-ready work within about 90 days. It depends on the time you put in — the phases are self-paced — but the path from first machine setup to studio-ready portfolio is mapped out for you.
